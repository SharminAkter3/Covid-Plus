What are the most common side effects after getting the vaccine?

After receiving the vaccine, common side effects may include mild discomfort such as soreness at the injection site, fatigue, headache, or mild fever. These are signs that your body is building protection against the virus. Side effects typically resolve within a few days.

When will vaccine be available to me?

Vaccine availability varies by location and priority groups set by health authorities. Check with your local health department or vaccination center for the most accurate and up-to-date information on when vaccines will be available to you.

Can I choose wich vaccine I get?

Vaccine distribution is managed by health authorities, and the type of vaccine available may depend on factors such as supply and distribution plans. It's essential to consult with healthcare professionals for guidance on vaccine options.

Can individuals with a history of allergies receive the COVID-19 vaccine?

Most individuals with allergies can safely receive the COVID-19 vaccine, but those with a history of severe allergic reactions should consult their healthcare provider beforehand.

Do I still need to wear a mask and socially distance after getting the vaccine?

Even after vaccination, it's crucial to continue following public health guidelines, including wearing masks and practicing social distancing, until a significant portion of the population is vaccinated and herd immunity is achieved.
